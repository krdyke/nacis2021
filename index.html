The WPA Map Collection

a snapshot of Oklahoma's rural land during the Great Depression and Dust Bowl

Property vs. sales vs. income tax. Sales tax first implemented to cover shortfall in 1933.
The Commission feels quite sure that the use of these maps will result in a much better equalization of real estate assessments in every county, and will further result in the placing on the tax rolls of a vast amount of improvements on real estate which have heretofore escaped taxation entirely.
https://digitalprairie.ok.gov/digital/collection/okresources/id/43472/rec/3

That's 2,313 maps.

They show the name of the owner and the assessed valuation of land and improvements separately for each tract of rural real estate within each county.... Land that is not assessed is clearly shown on the plats by use of diagonal lines.

Georeferencing the maps were an essential processing step. It was also much speedier than nearly any other georeferencing project, due to the structure of one map equaling one survey township.

We then used a model to clip out each map's marginalia.

With all the overlap removed, the individual maps for each county were combined into a mosaic.

Put them together and you have a continuous statewide layer.
